MENTAL HEALTH



Definition of Mental Health :

Mental health or mental health is the level of psychological well-being or mind free of disorders, “which is the psychological state of a person who has a good emotional and behavioral level.” From the perspective of positive psychology or a holistic view of mental health, it can include an individual's ability to enjoy life and create a balance between life's activities and demands to achieve psychological resilience.”


The most common type of mental illness:

  • Depression:

    • It is a state of sadness and unhappy feelings that control and control a person, making him a lazy person unable to perform any of his duties and tasks.



  • Autism or Autism Spectrum:

    • It is the inability to communicate with people and surrounding people, regardless of their proximity to him, and the tendency to isolate himself most of the time, and it often affects children in the early stages.



  • Bipolar disorder:

    • A state of mood swings between mania and depression, where a person experiences two different moods that greatly affect his psychological state.



  • Anxiety:

    • It is one of the disorders that most of the worlds population suffers from due to the circumstances and fluctuations they go through that affect the course of their lives, and it includes a group of other related disorders.



Psychological happiness:

The overall model of mental health generally includes concepts based on educational, psychological, religious, and humanitarian ideas, in addition to theoretical ideas from personality psychology, social, clinical, health, and developmental psychology.
